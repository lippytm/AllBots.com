# AllBots.com Quality Checklist

## Purpose

Keep the AllBots system clear, practical, organized, safe, and useful as a bot management concept.

## Clarity checklist

- [ ] The repo explains AllBots.com in plain English.
- [ ] The primary CTA is clear: Map Your Bot System.
- [ ] Bot roles are easy to understand.
- [ ] The system does not feel too complicated.
- [ ] The Canva card can be understood in 3 seconds.

## Bot system checklist

- [ ] Lead Bot role is defined.
- [ ] FAQ Bot role is defined.
- [ ] Sales Bot role is defined.
- [ ] Support Bot role is defined.
- [ ] Affiliate Bot role is defined.
- [ ] Follow-Up Bot role is defined.
- [ ] Training Bot role is defined.
- [ ] CRM and GitHub tracking are included.
- [ ] Human handoff is included where needed.

## Compliance checklist

- [ ] No guaranteed sales claims.
- [ ] No guaranteed lead claims.
- [ ] No guaranteed income claims.
- [ ] Affiliate/tool links are disclosed where used.
- [ ] Human review is included for important workflows.

## Canva quality checklist

- [ ] Headline readable on phone.
- [ ] CTA visible: Map Your Bot System.
- [ ] Bot role cards are not overcrowded.
- [ ] Flow from traffic to follow-up is clear.
- [ ] Exported in square, vertical, LinkedIn, and website sizes.

## GitHub quality checklist

- [ ] Bot role maps are saved in GitHub.
- [ ] Performance log is updated weekly.
- [ ] New bot role ideas become issues.
- [ ] Service offers are tied to clear outcomes.

## Best practice

AllBots.com should make automation feel organized, not overwhelming. Define roles before building tools.
